Problem

The $2.50 NYC congestion surcharge was introduced to reduce traffic and increase revenue, but its real-world application lacks consistency across time, zones, and trip distance. This has led to inefficiencies in both fairness and operations, with over-concentration in low-income zones and unclear triggers for drivers and city planners.

Action

  • Policy Pattern Analysis: Built classification models (Logistic Regression, Random Forest) to predict surcharge triggers (ROC-AUC 0.91); used SHAP to visualize key features like distance, time, and pickup zones
  • Revenue Impact Modeling: Used Ridge Regression to estimate the impact of variables on fare amounts, preparing groundwork for realistic scenario testing
  • Scenario Simulation: Simulated peak-hour adjustments and zone-based exceptions; compared revenue and equity outcomes across scenarios
  • Framework Design: Modularized simulation tools to allow reuse across pricing, logistics, and urban policy use cases

Result

  • +0.9% total revenue gain through optimized time + zone pricing strategies
  • Improved equity: Reduced surcharge overconcentration in underserved areas
  • Operational insight: Provided interpretable SHAP outputs for cross-functional policy and operations teams

Business Relevance

  • Transformed data insights into actionable policy proposals
  • Designed with imperfect data but focused on practical output
  • Delivered reusable logic for broader smart city and dynamic pricing strategies
