def get_delivery_group(days):
    if days <= 7:
        return 'Fast (<= 7 days)'
    elif 8 <= days <= 15:
        return 'Normal (8-15 days)'
    elif 16<= days <= 30:
        return 'Slow (16-30 days)'
    elif days > 30:
        return 'Very Slow (Long Tail)'
    else:
        return 'Unknown'

Retention Insight

This retention matrix clearly shows that Olist suffers from

  • (1) severe early churn,
  • (2) no improvement over time, and
  • (3) little impact from major eventsindicating a "leaky bucket" problem where users leave faster than they can be retained.

AARRR Funnel Analysis

This analysis helps identify where the biggest drop-off occurs in the customer journey.

  • Acquisition: Number of unique customer IDs
  • Activation: Number of customers with at least one successful delivery
  • Retention: Number of customers who placed more than two orders

Hypothesis 1: Is LTV related to delivery infrastructure?

Previous analysis showed large regional gaps in delivery performanceespecially delays in the North/Northeast of Brazil.

We test the reverse:
Are high-LTV (VIP) customers mostly located in major cities with better logistics (e.g., Southeast)?

If true, it would strongly support our claim that:

Poor delivery experiences reduce repeat purchases, while smooth delivery boosts LTV.

Hypothesis 2: Can delivery delay compensation improve retention?

Since poor logistics (an external factor) is a major cause of churn, we need short-term strategies to reduce its impact.

This hypothesis tests:
"If we proactively offer compensation (e.g., coupons) to customers with delayed orders, will it increase their repurchase rate?"

An A/B test to validate this will be part of our final proposal.

Hypothesis 2 Validation A/B Test: Retention via Delay Compensation

Does offering a 15% coupon to customers with delayed deliveries increase their repurchase rate compared to no compensation?

To estimate the impact, we build a repurchase prediction model and simulate an A/B test based on our hypothesis.

Limitation
We lack a variable indicating whether a coupon was applied, so we can't directly measure its effect.

Solution

  • We simulate the coupons impact by adjusting delivery time in the data:
    Based on our hypothesis that coupons reduce perceived delay, we reduce delivery time by 20% for the coupon group.
  • The model, trained to associate shorter delivery with higher repurchase, estimates upliftenabling ML-driven A/B test simulation.

Insight SMOTE's Limits in Real-World Testing

SMOTE balances training data, enabling better learning.
But since the test set remains imbalanced, minority-class recall stays low, and models like XGBoost tend to overfit.

To close the gap between training gains and real-world performance, we need:

  • Threshold tuning
  • Cost-sensitive learning

These are essential for practical improvement beyond SMOTE.
