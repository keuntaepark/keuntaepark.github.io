Problem

In a client project to visualize HVAC systems in Unity, we faced **performance bottlenecks** caused by overloaded high-resolution assets, leading to slow page loads and user drop-offs. A significant **lack of behavioral data** made UX direction unclear, and there was **conflicting expectations between design (visual fidelity) and engineering (performance and stability)**, hindering project alignment and progress.

Action

  • Product & Technical Bridging: Quickly learned HVAC system structure and industry context. Translated client requirements into precise technical specifications and effectively relayed them to the Unity development team, ensuring clear communication and shared understanding.
  • Behavioral Data Analysis for Optimization: Led data analysis using Python to generate heatmaps of user clicks and dwell time within the 3D virtual space. This data-driven insight allowed us to strategically lower texture resolution in underused 3D areas, directly improving loading speed and overall performance.
  • Stakeholder Alignment & Transparency: Created a real-time Miro dashboard as a single source of truth to share client feedback, issue tracking, and progress with all stakeholders. This proactive approach ensured transparency and fostered strong team alignment.
  • Data-Informed Design Trade-offs: Balanced design aesthetics (client priority) and system performance (engineering priority) by leveraging structured data analysis as objective evidence. This allowed us to find optimal compromise points and implement precise Unity scene configurations for maximum efficiency.

Result

  • Increased User Engagement & Lead Generation: Achieved a +100% visitor growth through improved visibility and a +15% session duration, indicating higher engagement and better UX.
  • Improved Technical Performance: Reduced page load time by 20%, significantly decreasing bounce rates and improving user satisfaction with a faster, smoother experience.
  • Enhanced Cross-Functional Collaboration: Successfully reconciled conflicting stakeholder expectations (design vs. engineering) by utilizing data as a neutral arbiter, leading to a more efficient and harmonized project execution.

PM Learnings & Future Vision

This project was a pivotal experience, teaching me how critical structured data is in resolving complex cross-functional conflicts. I successfully shifted from intuition-driven decisions to evidence-based alignment, a philosophy I now prioritize through early-stage measurement strategies in every subsequent project.

  • Proposed integration with Google Analytics and A/B testing to analyze behavior by user segment, enabling future personalized UX optimizations.
  • Suggested connecting UX improvements to conversion flows and sales outcomes, aiming to directly link product enhancements with measurable business value.
